OpenGL Project

❮ ❯

This was my 2nd year Graphics project where I learnt how to create an OpenGL game from scratch. I aimed to make an endless flying game in space where you fly through gates. The controls are first person with floating-like movement, as if you are moving in space. Once you pass through with a gate, the next gate appears and you gain a speed boost. Several asteroids are scattered around orbiting the planet at varying distances.

Notable features:

Ray plane intersection test with additional radius constraints to act as a circle collision in 3D space, to check player collision with the inside of the gate.

Realistic space flight using acceleration and drag.

  • Language: C++ with OpenGL

  • Date Completed: January 2025

  • Team Size: 1

Critical Exposure

❮ ❯

Critical Exposure is a first-person survival-horror game developed in Unreal Engine 5. Developed by a 2nd year team of 7, called "Occult Optics", at Falmouth University Games Academy.

You play a journalist investigating a abandand nuclear facility. Your aim is to explore the facility, taking photos, while avoiding a ghost located deep in the facility.

My Role:

Main gameplay features and the user interface using blueprints. Most notably taking photos and saving them to your camera roll and freezing the ghost with the camera.
